ABSTRACT
Abstract normally consists of four main parts. The first part of the abstract is a short introduction to the project that you have done. It is normally covered in 2 to 3 sentences. It is not to introduce what will be done, what has not been done and the objective of the project. Moreover, it is not a general introduction that is not from how you work in your project. The second part of the abstract is a brief and concise description of the project implementation. It is normally a summary from chapter’s details of the design and from system hardware and software. It can include the operation of your project product (for hardware or software type) in brief. Model numbers of specific or rare items (hardware or software) can be mentioned. This part is limited to 150 words. The third part of the abstract is a summary of important or significant results and discussion. The results or data and the discussion can be combined and presented in this part. Data/results can be mentioned in the form of relative manner, e.g. x is proportional to y with proportional constant of w or x = wy. Performance of hardware or software can be either quantitative or qualitative (descriptive) but the descriptive form should be result oriented. Important comparisons between theoretical and ideal cases and practical cases can also be included. The fourth part of the abstract is important or overall conclusion. Only the important or significant conclusions from chapter ‘conclusion’ are presented here. Alternatively, an overall conclusion which combines all the individual conclusions can be included here. Please note that the abstract CANNOT be included with the items such as: Issues related to personal, e.g. learned a lot of things from this project. First or second person pronouns e.g. I, we, you, me, my, etc. Outline of chapters in your project report. Any issues that are not produced from your project. Reference index or number.

Final year design project report must not exceed 100 pages including the appendices. The total number of pages for all relevant data sheets must not be more than 15 pages.

TEXT, FONT SIZE AND SPACING
Text should be typed, double spaced, left-right justified on one side of the paper only. TIMES NEW ROMAN font, font size of 12 point should be used throughout the report (except for Appendices), which includes Acknowledgement page, Abstract, Table of Content, List of Table, List of Figure and the main body of the text (which covers the main text, chapters, subtitles, headings, subheadings, figure captions, table captions, header and footers).
Single spacing can be use for references, captions of figure, long tables, table titles, long quotations, notes, footnotes, and multi-line caption.
For formulas, use equation editor (default setup is recommended) including subscripts and superscripts. Equations must be centered and numbered according to its sequences of order within the chapter.
A new paragraph at the bottom of a must have at least 2 full lines or it should begin on the next page.
SUBDIVISION
Text in each chapter can be divided under headings and sub-heading such as 1.1, 1.2,
1.3 etc and 1.1.1, 1.1.2, etc.
MARGINS
All text, tables, figures and their captions must conform to the following margins: Top	1 inch
Bottom	1 inch
Right	1 inch
Left	1.5 inch
PAPER AND PRINTING
High quality 80gm A4 size paper shall be used. The paper should be white in colour, acid free and ‘non-erasable’ kind. For printing, a laser printer or other high quality printer is recommended. Computer paper or carbon copies are not allowed.
PAGINATION
All Page numbers are without punctuation placed 1 cm from Bottom edge
Every page except the title page must be numbered.
Title page is ‘i’ but is not numbered. Preliminary page are to be numbered in lower case Roman numerals (ii, iii, ivetc).
The main text pages are to be numbered in Arabic numerals (1, 2, 3. etc) and all pages must be numbered consecutively and continuously.
CORRECTIONS
Correction of typing errors, or changes in the text, figures or tables, must be made as cleanly and invisibly as possible. Correction fluid shall NOT be used. Corrections should be made on the original before recopying or reprinting the problem page.
TABLES
Tables should be:
The caption of the table must be placed above the table.
Properly centered within the margin.
Numbered in relation to chapter. e.g. in Chapter 1, table should be numbered Table 1.1, Table 1.2, Table 2.1 and so on.
Numbered in Arabic numeral (Table 1.1, Table 1.2 etc) and a caption.
Number must correspond with text.
Place tables near to related discussion in text.
Use the word Table or TABLE.

FIGURE
Figures can be illustrations, graphs or anything other than tables, scripts and plates (photographs are regarded as plates). All figures must be placed near to related discussion in text. The figure can be refer using the word Figure or Fig. and numbered in Arabic numeral e.g. Fig. 1.1, Fig 1.2, Figure 2.1 and so on.
NOTES AND FOOTNOTES
Footnotes and notes are not encouraged and should be kept to a minimum. However, if required its font size must be smaller then general text.
USAGE OF COLOUR IN REPORT
The usage of colour in the reports should be minimized as much as possible. Some examples where colours should be avoided are Graphs, Tables, and Texts.
