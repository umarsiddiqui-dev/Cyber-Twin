Introduction:
       In today’s rapidly evolving digital landscape, cyberattacks are more frequent, complex, and damaging than ever before. Security teams and individual users often struggle to respond quickly and effectively to these incidents due to limited expertise, delayed reaction times, and fragmented tools. This project introduces CyberTwin — an AI-powered virtual assistant that operates in real time to support cybersecurity analysts and users during live cyberattacks.
      CyberTwin is designed to act as an intelligent companion (voice/text-based) that not only detects malicious activity but also recommends, explains, and helps execute countermeasures in real-time. By integrating threat intelligence, Natural Language Processing (NLP), and automation, CyberTwin empowers users to respond confidently and rapidly to threats, minimizing damage and downtime.

Problem Statement:
      Although Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) systems and antivirus software provide alerts and logs, they lack the contextual guidance or human-like advisory support during active attacks. In high-stress environments, even trained professionals may panic or take delayed action.
      There is currently no accessible and intelligent assistant that simplifies the cyber incident response process for both professionals and less experienced users by providing real-time instructions and executing tasks under approval. This leads to longer incident resolution times, higher financial losses, and increased vulnerability.
CyberTwin addresses this by acting as a copilot during cyberattacks, interpreting logs, cross-referencing threat databases (like MITRE ATT&CK), and giving voice- or text-based recommendations — making cyber defense proactive, guided, and far more efficient.

Features:
AI-Powered Cybersecurity Assistant:
Natural Language Processing (NLP)-based assistant that understands queries, responds with suggested actions, and guides users through incident resolution steps.
Real-Time Threat Monitoring & Response:
Detects suspicious activity using live logs and threat intelligence; suggests or automates appropriate actions based on severity.
Command Execution with User Approval:
Capable of running system-level commands (e.g., kill process, disable ports) upon user confirmation, reducing time to action during threats.
Customizable Incident Playbooks:
Embedded response workflows based on MITRE ATT&CK, adaptable to user environments or enterprise policies.

Dashboard & Chat Interface (Web or Electron App):
Provides a visual dashboard for alerts, user interactions, and action logs; chat interface for real-time query handling.
Voice or Text Interaction Modes:
Flexible input methods to enhance accessibility and usability under stressful conditions.
Threat Intelligence Integration:
Connects with free intelligence sources (MISP, MITRE, STIX) to provide context-aware recommendations.
Risk Scoring System:
Assesses and prioritizes incidents based on threat level, asset importance, and response history.
Simulated Attack Scenarios:
Uses tools like Atomic Red Team or CALDERA to practice and improve incident response capabilities in test environments.
